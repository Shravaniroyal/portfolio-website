Project Overview

Understanding the Challenge of Space Traffic Management

The Challenge

With the exponential rise in satellite deployments, particularly in Low Earth Orbit (LEO), space traffic management has emerged as one of the most pressing challenges in modern aerospace operations. Our project addresses the critical need for proactive orbital congestion prediction.

Our Solution

We developed a comprehensive machine learning framework that analyzes historical satellite data to predict future traffic density patterns, enabling better mission planning and collision avoidance strategies.

Data Sources

Space-Track.org, CelesTrak, NASA ODPO

ML Models

Decision Tree, Random Forest, LSTM

Performance

89.3% Accuracy with Decision Tree

Methodology

Our Data-Driven Approach to Space Traffic Analysis

Data Collection & Preprocessing

Aggregated orbital data from multiple sources including TLE datasets, satellite catalogs, and debris tracking systems. Cleaned and standardized over 25,000 orbital entries spanning 10 years.

  • Space-Track.org TLE data parsing
  • CelesTrak satellite catalogs
  • Data validation and cleaning

Exploratory Data Analysis

Conducted comprehensive EDA to understand spatial and temporal patterns in orbital traffic, identifying congestion hotspots and seasonal trends.

  • Altitude distribution analysis
  • Temporal traffic pattern identification
  • Spatial clustering visualization

Feature Engineering

Transformed raw orbital parameters into meaningful features for machine learning models, including derived metrics and temporal features.

  • Orbital zone classification (LEO/MEO/GEO)
  • Traffic density binning
  • Time-series feature creation

Model Development & Training

Implemented and compared multiple ML algorithms, optimizing for accuracy and interpretability in space traffic density prediction.

  • Decision Tree Classifier (89.3% accuracy)
  • Random Forest ensemble methods
  • LSTM for temporal dependencies
